One out of every three cups of coffee consumed globally is linked to Brazil, the world's largest coffee exporter. Among the top five coffee-producing countries —Brazil, Vietnam, Colombia, Indonesia, and Ethiopia— each brings unique qualities to the global coffee market. Brazil, for instance, exports about 90% high-quality Arabica beans, a staple in premium espresso blends. Similarly, Vietnam leads in robusta production, while Colombia is renowned for its smooth, balanced Arabica. Ethiopia, the birthplace of coffee, offers rich, diverse flavors, and Indonesia is famed for specialty coffees like Sumatra and Java. While all these nations have the capacity to export roasted, value-added coffee, green coffee beans still dominate exports. Expanding processed coffee exports across these leading producers could provide consumers with greater access to their unique flavors and promote value addition within producing countries. Each country also boasts diverse coffee-growing regions, varying in altitude, varieties, and cultivation systems. For example, Brazil's regions range from high-altitude, handpicked farms to mechanized flatlands. In contrast, Ethiopia's coffee often grows wild or semi-wild under forest canopies, while Colombia's coffee thrives on steep Andean slopes. Supporting the processed coffee industries in these countries offers global consumers not only enhanced flavor options but also a way to empower millions of smallholder coffee producers whose livelihoods depend on the crop.

Jamaican Blue Mountain

Known for its smooth, mild flavor, and lack of bitterness, Jamaican Blue Mountain coffee is one of the most sought-after and expensive coffees globally. It’s grown in the Blue Mountains of Jamaica, at high altitudes, with ideal growing conditions.

Hawaiian Kona Coffee

Grown on the slopes of the volcanoes in Hawaii, Kona coffee has a smooth, rich flavor with hints of nuttiness and chocolate. It’s known for its full-bodied taste and low acidity.

Ethiopian Yirgacheffe

Known as the birthplace of coffee, Ethiopia produces some of the best coffee beans in the world. Yirgacheffe coffee is highly prized for its floral notes, light body, and bright acidity. It’s considered one of the most complex coffees.

Panama Geisha (Gesha)

Panama’s Geisha beans, especially from the Boquete region, are often considered among the best coffee beans in the world due to their unique, fruity, and floral taste profile. Geisha is famous for its complexity, with flavors ranging from jasmine to tropical fruits and honey.

Colombian Coffee

Colombia is one of the top coffee producers globally, and its beans are known for their balanced flavors, medium body, and bright acidity. Colombian coffee offers a variety of taste profiles, but it is typically smooth, with hints of caramel, citrus, and chocolate.

Sumatra Mandheling

Sumatra coffee from Indonesia is known for its earthy, full-bodied flavor with low acidity and distinctive herbal, spicy notes. The beans are often processed using the wet-hulling method, giving them their signature rich and bold taste.

SCosta Rican Tarrazu

Coffee from the Tarrazu region of Costa Rica is well-regarded for its bright acidity, smooth body, and complex flavors. It often features notes of citrus, chocolate, and honey and is loved by those who appreciate a clean and crisp cup.
